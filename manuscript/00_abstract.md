# Abstract {-}

We develop a formal account of *policy entanglement* in active inference (AIF) agents that maintain multiple concurrent policy variables — across modalities, time horizons, or inference modes (variational vs. expected free energy). Beginning from the standard discrete-time POMDP active inference setting with likelihood matrix $A$, transition tensor $B$, and policy prior $E$, we introduce a single scalar coupling parameter $\lambda \in [0,\infty)$ that smoothly deforms the joint policy posterior away from the mean-field product baseline ($\lambda = 0$) toward arbitrary structured joint distributions ($\lambda \to \infty$). We derive a clean *entanglement decomposition* of variational free energy ([[THMREF:thm_4_1]]) into a sum of marginal free energies, a coupling-potential expectation, and a strictly non-positive total-correlation (multi-information) term — formalizing the agentic gain of joint policy structure. We embed this in dual e/m information geometry, prove the family $\{q_\lambda\}$ traces an exponential geodesic away from the mean-field submanifold ([[THMREF:thm_6_4]]), and connect Schmidt rank / tensor-network rank of the joint policy posterior to the "archetypal eigenvectors" of the entanglement structure — biomimetically interpretable as the few dominant cross-stream behavioral modes that an agent's hyperprior repeatedly sculpts. We give an $O(\lambda^2)$ suboptimality bound ([[THMREF:thm_8_1]]) for VFE-only streams operating within EFE-planning ensembles, recovering hierarchical active inference as a sparse-coupling limit, with connections to copula variational inference [@tran-2015], products of experts [@hinton-2002], options frameworks [@sutton-1999], sophisticated inference [@friston-2021], branching-time AIF [@champion-2022], renormalization-group active inference [@friston-2024], and KL/path-integral control [@theodorou-todorov-2012; @kappen-2005]. We provide a phased Lean formalization plan with concrete definitions, lemmas, and theorems compatible with mathlib4 — the boundary fragment is fully type-checked with `lake build` green and 10 documented `sorry` placeholders awaiting Mathlib refinement (Phase 7) — a worked $K=2$ Bernoulli toy with closed-form $\lambda^*$ verified by 119 numerical tests at 98% coverage, and a roadmap for empirical investigation of phase transitions, archetype learning, and hyperprior revertibility. A unified notation glossary ([[SECREF:notation]]) consolidates every symbol used in the paper. The framework is designed to be theoretically novel, biologically interpretable, and machine-checkable.

**Keywords:** active inference, free energy principle, policy inference, mean-field, total correlation, information geometry, e/m-flatness, tensor networks, Schmidt rank, sophisticated inference, hierarchical RL, KL control, Lean theorem proving, formal verification.
